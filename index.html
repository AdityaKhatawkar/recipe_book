Tea is one of the most popular beverages in the world, cherished for its diverse flavors, cultural significance, and potential health benefits. Originating in ancient China, tea has a rich history that spans thousands of years, evolving into various styles and traditions across different cultures. Tea is celebrated not only for its refreshing qualities but also for its potential health benefits. Many studies suggest that tea is rich in antioxidants, which may help reduce the risk of chronic diseases, promote heart health, and support weight management. Herbal teas, made from a variety of plants, flowers, and spices, offer their own unique benefits and flavors, ranging from calming chamomile to invigorating peppermint.

Ingredients:

  • 1 cup of water.
  • 1 teaspoon of tea leaves (or 1 tea bag).
  • 1-2 teaspoons of sugar
  • 1-2 teaspoons of milk

Instructions

Boil Water:
Pour 1 cup of water into a small pot or kettle and bring it to a boil.
Add Tea Leaves:
Once the water starts boiling, reduce the heat to a simmer and add 1 teaspoon of tea leaves (or use a tea bag).
Let the tea steep for 2-3 minutes (adjust based on how strong you like your tea).
Add Sugar (Optional):
If you like your tea sweet, add 1-2 teaspoons of sugar to the boiling water while the tea steeps. Stir until dissolved.
Strain the Tea:
Remove the tea from heat and strain it into a cup (or simply remove the tea bag if you used one).
Add Milk (Optional):
If you prefer milk tea, pour in a small amount of milk (1-2 teaspoons). Stir it in.
Optional Variations:
For lemon tea, add a slice of lemon instead of milk.
For spiced tea (Chai), add a pinch of cardamom, cinnamon, or ginger while boiling the water.
